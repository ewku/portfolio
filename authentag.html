Authentag

User research project for an early stage startup bringing the world of IoT to MedTech industry. Enterprise software.

Background

In 2016 the FDA released new medical device tracking requirements. These new rules created a need for an efficient tracking system that Medtech manufacturers can rely on. With 6,500 medical device companies in the United States, Authentag saw a business opportunity in providing tracking for the unique identification required by each device.


Client

Authentag

Role

User Research, Wireframing

Date

March, 2017

Challenge

Identify opportunities in medical devices industry for Authentag’s unique identification tracking technology and translate them into design concepts.

Result

Conducted interviews with medical professionals and manufacturers revealed poor practices in maintaining medical devices documents and history which in many cases would lead to significant financial losses. Suggested solutions included: A mobile application concept simplifying logging each device’s history and ownership at each stage of its life by scanning its unique code with a phone and storing in a cloud; A dashboard concept with clear overview of each device’s lifecycle and easy access to all documents to support easy FDA’s auditing processes.
